Twelve Pillars

My Summary on Chapter 4-7

All 4 chapters were focusing on sacrifices that one must make to achieve one’s goals, the first chapter (4) spoke mainly about you as an individual setting goals and procedures on how you’ll ensure that you reach them. Chapter (5) mentioned the basics of managing your time for the best possible after results. Next chapter (6) mentioned that for you to have growth there is a requirement for you to surround yourself with person of high/higher quality that you can compare yourself with to encourage you to work harder toward your goals, rather than been associated with people who seemed no help on your future. And the last chapter (7) reasoned the importance of self-education and how it exposes you to different categories of knowledge.

My reflection on Twelve Pillars Chapters (4-7)

The chapters were very much an eye-opener for me, especially chapter 4 and 5 as I noticed that they had an instant impact on me and my current position in life. Reading through both motivated me to grab a pen and jotted down some goals that I intend to achieve in the coming months and maybe years as I’ll be adding more. The line that stated “We must all suffer from one of two pains: The pain of discipline or the pain of regret. The difference is discipline weighs ounces while regret weighs tons.” As since recently there have been a lot of regrets in my life pertaining to my academics’ results and what could have been done to prevent such. So now going forward I’ll now be using this a quote to guide me in the right direction.
